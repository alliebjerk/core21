# Brand Voice

## Voice Descriptors

Direct. Warm but not soft. Funny when it fits. Tells the truth even when the truth is inconvenient. Zero corporate. Zero hype. Sounds like a smart friend who also happens to be very good at business — not like a brand.

If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it. If it sounds like a TED Talk, rewrite it. If a stranger would read it and say "oh, she actually gets it" — keep it.

## Sentence Structure Rules

- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Contractions always. "Don't" not "do not." "You're" not "you are."
- One idea per paragraph. Two sentences max before a line break.
- Questions are okay. Rhetorical is fine. But don't stack more than two in a row.
- No em dashes in emails or social. Fine in long-form if truly necessary — but use sparingly.
- Never start with "I'm so excited to share" or any variation.
- Never end with "Let me know if you have questions!" Use a full stop or a CTA. Not both.
- Lead with the thing that matters. No preamble.
- Active voice. Always.
- Numbers beat adjectives. "$37" beats "affordable." "Three steps" beats "a few simple steps."

## Banned Words and Phrases

**Corporate / buzzword:**
synergy, leverage (as a verb), unlock, level up, game-changer, paradigm, cutting-edge, innovative, revolutionary, next-level, deep dive, circle back, bandwidth, touch base, reach out (use "email me" or "DM me"), actionable, robust

**Toxic positivity / hype:**
boss babe, queen, slay, crush it, hustle, grind, killing it, you've got this, manifest, abundance mindset (as a standalone concept), girl boss, empowered, empower (the verb), "I'm so excited to share," "I'm obsessed," "literally obsessed," "So good!", "This is EVERYTHING."

**Fake-honest openers:**
"In today's world," "Now more than ever," "Let's be honest," "Here's the thing," "The truth is," "Full transparency," "I'm going to be real with you" — these signal the honest thing is coming. Just say the honest thing.

**Clichés:**
"at the end of the day," "move the needle," "low-hanging fruit," "think outside the box," "on the same page," "it is what it is"

## Signature Phrases

- **"Tiny Offer"** / **"Tiny Offer® Method"** — core methodology (use ® in formal mentions)
- **"The math isn't mathing"** — when something doesn't add up strategically
- **"Build it on yourself first"** — core philosophy on offers and systems
- **"That's not a strategy, that's a wish"** — calling out vague plans
- **"Self-Made"** — the membership/community (capital S, capital M, always)
- **"Prosperity Lab"** — the LLC (formal/legal contexts only, not marketing)

## Opening Patterns

**Email subject lines:**
- Lead with the result, the number, or the counterintuitive thing
- Specific questions work: "What happens after the tiny offer?" beats "Do you want more sales?"
- No fake urgency. No "Re:" tricks.

**Email body openers:**
- Start with a scene, a number, or the point. No warmup paragraph.
- Good: "Your first tiny offer doesn't have to be perfect. It has to exist."
- Bad: "Hey! I've been thinking about you and wanted to pop into your inbox..."

**Social captions:**
- Hook = first line only. Must stop the scroll.
- Never start with "I" on Instagram (hidden in the preview).

## Closing Patterns

- CTAs are direct. "Grab it here." "Join Self-Made." "DM me 'tiny.'"
- One CTA per email. Not three.
- Social: CTA or question. Not both.

## Emotional Range

Allie's voice can be:
- **Dry and funny** — the default register for social
- **Direct and firm** — correcting a misconception or calling out bad advice
- **Warm and real** — personal stories, behind-the-scenes, member wins
- **Excited but measured** — launching something; energy shows, exclamation points don't

She is NOT: preachy, inspirational-poster-y, performatively vulnerable, or self-deprecating to the point of undermining.

## Platform-Specific Adjustments

**Instagram:** Conversational. Hook on line 1. Don't start with "I." CTA goes to DM keyword or link in bio.

**Email:** Longer is fine if every line earns its place. One CTA. Reads like she's writing to one person.

**Sales pages:** More structure, more proof, more specificity. Still her voice — not "professional" copy that sounds like someone else.

**Slack (team):** Casual. Emoji used purposefully, not as punctuation.
